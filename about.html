Origins

Formed in Seattle, Washington in 1994 by former Nirvana member Dave Grohl, the Foo Fighters started as a one-man project after the tragic suicide of Nirvana lead singer Kurt Cobain. The debut Album "Foo Fighters" featured Dave Grohl as the only official member.

The current members of the band include Dave Grohl 1994-present (lead vocals, guitar), Nate Mendel 1995-present (bass), Pat Smear 1995-1997, 2010-present (guitar, backing vocals), Taylor Hawkins 1997-present (drums, percussion, backing vocals), Chris Shiflett 1999-present (guitar, backing vocals), and Rami Jaffee 2017-present (keyboard, piano). Former members include William Goldsmith 1995-1997 (drums, percussion), and Franz Stahl 1997-1999 (guitar, backing vocals).

Supported Charities

The Foo Fighters have spent many years supporting various charities, including the American Civil Liberties Union (ACLU) of Southern California, a charity dedicated to fighting anti-immigration policies and rampant police brutality, and LIFEbeat, a charity formed in 2009 to help young people develop the skills they will need as well as inspiring them.
The band has also made statements within their music supporting the LGBTQ community, including performing dressed as long haired cowboys to perform an unannounced show which was protested by the Westboro Baptists Church, specifically playing their song "Keep It Clean" which video and lyrics desribes love between two men. In 2015 they performed in the annual fundraiser in San Francisco for Dreamforce, helping to raise $10 million for a Children's Hospital.
